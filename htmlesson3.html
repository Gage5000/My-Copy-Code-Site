HTML Quotation and Citation Tags

HTML has several tags for defining different Quotations and Citations.

The Blockquote Tag

The <blockquote> tag defines a section that is quoted from another source.

Most of the time blockquotes are indent to show that it is a quote.

You can add a Attribute called cite so that you can add the website is that you are quoting.

<blockquote cite="https://www.noodles.com/about-noodles">Since 1995, our mission at Noodles and Company has been to nourish and inspire every team member, guest and community we serve.</blockquote>
Since 1995, our mission at Noodles and Company has been to nourish and inspire every team member, guest and community we serve.

The Q Tag

The <q> tag defines a short quote and is short for quotation.

It adds quotations marks around the quote.

<p>In this quote Noodles and Company tells there mission saying<q> our mission at Noodles and Company has been to nourish and inspire every team member, guest and community we serve.</q></p>

In this quote Noodles And Company tells there mission saying our mission at Noodles and Company has been to nourish and inspire every team member, guest and community we serve.


The Abbr Tag

The <abbr> tag defines an abbreviation or an acronym, like "HTML", "CSS", and "ASAP" abbreviation or "Dr.", "Mr", "Ms" acronym.

The abbr tag works with the title attribute to show the description for the abbreviation/acronym when you mouse over the text.

For this example to work we need to use the & so we use &amp; to do so.

<p>I would love to go to</abbr title="Noodles and Company"> N&C</abbr></p>

I would love to go to N&C


The Address Tag

The <address> tag defines contact information for the author or owner of a document or an article.

The text in the address tag are italic, and browsers add a line break before and after the address tag.

<address>

Written by Joe Stavens.<br>

Visit us at:<br>

Example.com<br>

Box 173, landpark<br>

USA

</address>

The Cite Tag

The <cite> tag defines the name of a art work like books, poems, songs, movies, sculptures, etc.

The text in the cite tag are in italic.

<p> The movie <cite> How the Grinch Stole Christmas</cite>director by Ron Howard and released on Nov 17, 2000</p>

The movie How the Grinch Stole Christmas director by Ron Howard and released on Nov 17, 2000


The Bdo Tag

The <bdo> tag overrides the current text direction revasing the text.

bdo stands for Bi-Directional Override.

The dir attribute is what is changing the direction that it is going with rtl reversing it and ltr doing nothing.

<bdo dir="rtl">This shows what bdo is doing.</bdo>

<bdo dir="ltr">This shows what bdo is doing.</bdo>
This shows what bdo is doing. This shows what bdo is doing.
